Tree Pruning

While the trees in the wild can survive with natural pruning, the trees in the urban outfits face a dearth of all the nutrients and have to face stiff competition from the other plants and shrubs growing in the area.

Why Do You Need Professional Tree Pruning Services?

Though pruning looks like a cakewalk, improper or over-pruning may do more harm than good. Besides, the location and type of cut has a huge impact on how the tree grows in the future. In such a scenario, it is more than necessary that the tree pruning process is left in the hands of a professional and recognized tree running company.

Besides, tall trees may have both sturdy and brittle branches so it is quite unsafe to do it yourself due to the possibility of injuries. Professional tree pruners will employ all the safety measures so that no one gets harmed. 

Another reason to choose tree pruning services is that we are masters of the craft and thus are well versed with all the best practices for pruning trees. The following are a few of the techniques we employ:

  1. Cleaning - Cleaning involves the simple removal of dead or dried branches, which may be susceptible to fall in the next storm or wind, mostly from the crown of the tree.
  2. Thinning - Thinning is the process in which we remove branches selectively, mostly for reasons like structuring, branches growing in wrong directions. It also helps in improving the health of the tree by increasing the air circulation and access to sunlight for all the branches.
  3. Raising - Raising involves increasing the ground clearance of the lower part of the tree. For example, the branches may be outgrowing close to the pedestrian path, or are low enough to collide with the rooftop of vehicles or the pedestrians on foot.
  4. Reduction - Reduction involves reducing the height of the tree, generally executed to clear space for overhead utility lines, or other architectural needs.  

The Best Time to Choose Tree Pruning Services

The basic need for pruning is to get rid of weak or dead limbs of the tree that may still be consuming the essential nutrients. Though pruning can be best performed right before spring breaks, the process of pruning can be carried out at any time of the year, or season. The only difference is that the onset of spring will ensure the maximum and speedier growth of new branches than in the other parts of the year.

Also, care has to be taken that if the tree has diseased branches, pruning must not be done during the active transmission seasons. This is because the disease will readily spread to the rest of the tree through the pruning wounds, dance proving harmful to the tree. However, it is not always advisable to wait for the spring, depending on the condition of the tree.
